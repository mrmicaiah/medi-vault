1. Virginia Barrier Crimes Act

Pursuant to Virginia Code § 19.2-392.02 and § 32.1-162.9:1, certain criminal convictions ("barrier crimes") may prohibit employment in positions that involve direct contact with individuals receiving home care services.

2. Barrier Crimes Include

Under the Code of Virginia, barrier crimes include but are not limited to:

  • Murder, manslaughter, or malicious wounding
  • Assault and battery (certain offenses)
  • Sexual offenses, including rape, sexual battery, and offenses involving minors
  • Robbery, burglary, and arson
  • Drug-related felonies (manufacture, distribution, or possession with intent)
  • Fraud and financial exploitation offenses
  • Abuse, neglect, or exploitation of incapacitated adults or children
  • Any substantially similar offenses under the laws of another state or the federal government

3. Disclosure Obligation

I understand that I am required to disclose whether I have ever been convicted of or are currently charged with any barrier crime as defined by the Code of Virginia. I further understand that:

  1. The Agency is required by Virginia law to conduct a criminal background check before hiring.
  2. Conviction of a barrier crime may permanently disqualify me from employment in a direct care position.
  3. Failure to disclose a barrier crime conviction may result in immediate termination and potential legal consequences.
  4. Certain barrier crimes have a defined "look-back" period, while others constitute a permanent bar to employment.

4. Attestation

I hereby attest that:

  • I have read and understand the barrier crimes provisions of the Code of Virginia.
  • I have disclosed all relevant criminal history to the Agency as part of my application.
  • I understand that the Agency will conduct a background check as required by Virginia law.
